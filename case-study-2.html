The Evolution of Scalling: From Micrometer to Nanometer

Evolution of semiconductor scaling from micrometer to nanometer technology has driven the digital revolution for the past five decades.

Moore's Law and Early Scaling

Guided by Moore's Law, the industry has successfully doubled the number of transistors on a chip approximately every two years. In the early days, scaling from micrometers simply involved shrinking the transistor dimensions linearly, a concept known as Dennard Scaling, which allowed for faster switching speeds and lower power consumption per transistor.

The Nanometer Era Challenges

As features scaled down into the nanometer regime (below 100nm), physical limitations such as short-channel effects, quantum tunneling, and current leakage became prominent. This necessitated a shift away from planar transistors to more complex architectures like FinFETs and, more recently, Gate-All-Around (GAA) structures.

Looking Forward

Today, scaling is no longer just about shrinking dimensions but also involves advanced packaging (chiplets), 3D integration, and adopting new materials. The journey from micrometer to nanometer perfectly encapsulates the relentless innovation within the semiconductor industry.
